How it works

How it works in 3 steps

Step 1

Capture proposed change

An engineer or AI agent proposes a code change. Engram detects what services and dependencies are touched.

Step 2

Assemble real context

Engram pulls related incidents, PRs, ADRs, and ownership signals into one packet, so reviewers do not have to reconstruct context manually.

Step 3

Guide safe action

Teams get clear risk guidance: proceed, review, or pause, helping catch risky changes before production impact.

Policy Outcomes

Action decisions mapped to explicit risk conditions

Allow

Low-risk change, no recent incident links, owner verified.

Warn

Moderate uncertainty or incomplete context; proceed with caution.

Review

High-impact service or recent incident history requires owner review.

Block

Destructive or production-sensitive action without required approvals.

Trust Boundaries

Built to reduce risk, not automate blindly

  • Engram reduces risk; it does not guarantee perfect diagnosis.
  • Critical actions remain human-approved by policy.
  • Incident recommendations are runbook-grounded and evidence-linked.
  • No unsupported root-cause claims without traceable evidence.
